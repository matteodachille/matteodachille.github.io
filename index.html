Welcome to my personal webpage.


Since 10/2020 I am postdoctoral research and teaching assistant (ATER) at Laboratoire d’Analyse et de Mathématiques Appliquées, UMR CNRS 8050 (Paris-XII).

Previously I have been associated member at Laboratoire d'Informatique de Paris Nord, UMR CNRS 7030 (Paris-XIII). On Friday, October 10th, 2020 I defended my PhD thesis at the Université Paris-Saclay under the co-direction of William Jalby, Olivier Rivoire and Andrea Sportiello, working on Euclidean Random Assignment Problems. More details on my PhD thesis are available here.


Besides Euclidean Random Assignment Problems, my research interests include other topics in statistical mechanics of lattice systems, such as long-range Ising models, rotator models, in particular properties of Gibbs measures under the action of Renormalization Group. Further details are available at the scientific interests page.
